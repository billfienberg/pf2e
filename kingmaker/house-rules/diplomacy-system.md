The NPC being negotiated will have 4 components.  Interest, patience, motivations, and pitfalls.  Interest is how eager they are to make a deal.  Patience is how much time and effort they're willing to negotiate for.  Motivations are what drive the NPC.  Pitfalls are the opposite of motivations, things they fear or had disdain for.

The goal is to more connect real life interaction for negotiation.

This is how players interact with the system.

Before a negotiation you can put effort into learning motivations and pitfalls of the target npc.  You could also learn their interest and patience, but it's more likely you'll know those intuitively.

During the negotiation you want to raise their interest enough to get what you want, before their patience runs out.  You do this by aligning what you want from the npc with their motivations, while avoiding their pitfalls.  If you address their motivations, you can raise their interest in you, but if you address a pitfall it will lower their interest.  If you reason with them without aligning their motivation, it's just harder to convince them.

Is there anything for you to do? Not really. Just be aware that NPCs have motivations. They have likes and dislikes.  It allows everyone to participate, not just the diplomatic characters.  If you strongly appeal a motivation, you might not even need to make any rolls.  And you can work together as a group.  This is just to let you know  that behind the scenes, I am keeping track of it and making sure there are motivations and pitfalls for you to interact with.

Example:

A wild druid might seek peace.  They want a calm life they live to themselves.  If you want them to assist in a ritual, you can look at how that ritual can help their peaceful life.  If you help us now, the disease of the forest won't reach this area and you'll be left alone.  Or you can offer something.  You could offer a contract to the land so if you claim it you won't build on it.
